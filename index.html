Outstanding Orthodontist: No More Artifactual Teeth in Talking Face

Zibo Su, Ziqi Zhang, Kun Wei*, Xu Yang, Cheng Deng

Xidian University

Teaser

We introduce OrthoNet, a plug-and-play module composed of a Detail-oriented Teeth Aligner and a Memory-guided Teeth Stabilizer, which effectively removes artifactual teeth and maintains cross-frame consistency in audio-driven talking face synthesis.

Abstract

Audio-driven talking face methods often suffer from artifactual or inconsistent teeth, degrading realism and temporal coherence. We propose OrthoNet, a lightweight, plug-and-play framework that (1) aligns the dental region with shape-aware, detail-preserving cues and (2) stabilizes teeth appearance through a long–short term memory design, yielding natural and temporally consistent results across diverse identities and speaking conditions. Extensive experiments show OrthoNet reduces artifacts and improves perception metrics over strong baselines, without sacrificing lip–audio synchronization. Code and models will be released.
